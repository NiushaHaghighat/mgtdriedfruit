Raisin

Raisins are dried grapes. These grapes are initially green in skin color and darken as they dry, becoming a dense, dark-colored dried fruit containing small seeds and bursting with sweet flavor. Raisins are being produced in the U.S., Turkey, Iran, Greece and Australia. Iran is the third largest exporter of raisin in the world by exporting more than 150,000 MT (27% global Consumption) of raisins worth around 250 million dollars annually. Some of famous Raisin types exported by MGT Dried Fruit: Sultana Light Raisin, Sultana Dark Sun Dried Raisin, Golden Raisin, Green Jumbo and Golden Jumbo Raisin.

Pistachio

Pistachio trees originally come from the Middle East, especially Persia (Iran). Not only are pistachio nuts tasty and fun to eat, they are also super healthy. Pistachio Vera tree contain healthy fats and are a good source of protein, fiber and antioxidants. They also contain several essential nutrients and have benefits for weight loss, as well as heart and gut health. Pistachios are used in many happy events as a luxuriant dried fruits and nutritious ingredient in main foods. Pistachio Types: Ahmad Aghaei, Kaleh Ghochi, Fandoghi and Akbari. Almond

Almond is known as Badam in Iran, Afghanistan, India and Pakistan. Badam (Almond) is one of Middle East and south Asia species fruits, which consists of an outer hull and a hard shell that the seed is hidden inside the shell. Normally Buyers are interested in buying either shelled or unshelled almonds, obviously shelled almonds are more expensive as the shells have been taken off and it has become the final edible almond.

Saffron

Ancient Persians cultivated Persian saffron by the 10th century BC. Most of the world’s saffron will provide from Iran, which is the world capital of saffron. Iran provides 97% of high-quality saffron production. Saffron contains several plant-derived chemical compounds that are known to have been antioxidant, disease preventing, and health promoting properties.
